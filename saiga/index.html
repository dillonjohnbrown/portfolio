Saiga Case Study - Product Design

This page lays out my ideas for a machine-learning powered app designed to help people easily apply for a German visa.

Outline

  1. Download App
  2. Onboard
    1. Reassurance
      • Success stories, security of user data, app origin story
      • Carefully chosen words to put user at ease and fill with feeling of empowerment a
    2. Preliminary Questions
      • User answers questions to determine what kind of visa is needed
      • Redirect users we can't help b
    3. Physical Item Checklist
      • Checklist for physical items to be gathered
      • Assist user to acquire items not already in their possession c
  3. Data Entry
    1. Scan physical documents with camera d
    2. User manually enters any remaining data
  4. Review
    1. Final review of all info with missing fields highlighted
  5. Submit
    1. App submits as many forms submitted digitally as possible
    2. Paper forms are printed and mailed as part of our service e
    3. Option to save documents outside of app
  6. Other Applications (spouse, dependents, etc.)
    1. Begin from step 1.2
    2. Option to re-use data from previous applications
  7. Instructions
    1. Checklist of appointments and locations for tasks outside of the app f

Notes

  1. e.g. "We can do this!"
  2. Every user needs to have a positive experience with our app, even if our service can't help.
  3. e.g. if a user does not yet have a passport, we provide a link to the appropriate government website to apply for one
  4. This is the lynchpin to the entire app feeling effortless. Common government forms, ID cards, and passports can be scanned with a smartphone. Data is categorized and used to complete applications.
  5. Forms that must be physically mailed are sent to government bodies by us. Receipts and/or documents that must be in user's possession are mailed to the user. The user doesn't need to touch a piece of paper unless required by law or they choose to complete tasks by hand.

    Maybe this begins as a manual process of us stuffing envelopes, but ideally this will be automated.
  6. e.g. "Take your passport, a credit card, and the packet you received from Saiga to Rathausstraße 15 10178 Berlin."

User Flow & MVP Mockup

This is the high-level user flow of the app. We want our users to scan their documents and leave the tedious work to software. Users who do not scan any documents have to enter in their data by hand, but still get a better, less error-prone experience than using paper.

This is my minimum viable product mockup.g The app is primarily inputs for the user to populate with personal details. Our target persona, Raquel, wants to live and work in Berlin but she is flustered by boring bureaucratic paperwork.h

These forms need a lot of information. Instead of telling Raquel to manually answer every single question, our app makes the application process magical with a couple of photos. The app scans passports, tax forms, ID cards, and more. The app then uses the information found to automate as much of the application as possible.

Because the app is focused on obtaining a German visa, we know exactly what data is needed to complete these forms. We can find the most popular expat countries and pre-load their common forms into our app.i This magic extends to outside the app as well. If the app detects from your scans that you are a Canadian diplomat, you get different instructions and rules than a Brazilian student.

All Raquel does is snap some photos and answer a few questions, then we handle the rest. Important dates are added to her calendar app. In-person appointments are booked, and we give her directions to get to them. We submit her application and mail her a packet with everything she'll need in her meetings.

Notes

  1. Made with Sketch and Apple's iOS UI library.
  2. Raquel and I have a lot in common.
  3. The aim is to make the software smarter. By training the app with popular forms, it will know the difference between a W-2 from the USA and the equivalent from Ireland, and it knows where the relevant information is within them.

    Less common forms make the model smarter too. Utility bills, bank statements, birth certificates and more will all make the app better and feel more magical. The goal is to allow users to scan anything they have at hand.

Inspiration

I took inspiration from a website Raquel may frequent, The Balance Careers. This site helps young professionals succeed in their careers. The Balance Careers has a unique style and an empowering spirit that I thought would be right at home in my design.

I also took inspiration from a folder on my computer full of graphic design, art, and other images I find admirable in some way.

And finally I took inspiration from government forms. This provided me with examples of what to avoid.

In Closing

Some shortcuts I took were around my key feature, the document scanner. Taking photos of important documents could be a security risk if we store or transmit sensitive data like this, so I imagined everything taking place locally on the users device, and any saved data would be stored securely. Also, I assumed Raquel understands and trusts the software enough to scan her documents.

Another shortcut is in regards to the visa forms. I only glanced at a few of the forms and some of the instructions provided by the German government. This just about guarantees that I oversimplified something.

I would spend more time with other people if this were a real assignment. Real people, whiteboards, and lots of papers mapping out this beast. Because the consequences of messing up someone's visa application are real, I would need as many eyes on this as possible. Maybe even consultation with a paralegal to help.

This was a fun project. Thinking through a process with this much complexity was challenging and absorbing, for sure. I had a hard time letting go when I wanted to dig into details. Sometimes I got lost in minutiae, and other times I worried my take was a little too absract. Still, I hope this has given you an idea of how I work. I even had ideas that I couldn't fit into this document, like payment (my 2 cents: users don't pay unless the app works).
