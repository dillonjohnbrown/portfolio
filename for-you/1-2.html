1.2 (SECOND TIME)

The bed is a mess, our clothes strewn in a path to the bedroom. We pant, exhausted, sweating, and blissful. Finally the last of our walls are let down. The pillows have never felt so soft. I brush your hair from your face. You respond with crescent eyes and an easy smile. Eyes prickle with water out of happiness.

For a long time we don't say a word — we don't need to. Laying together, nose to nose, our eyes say everything. We touch, we laugh, our eyes half-open. One of us finally says something silly and we howl.

Warmth, acceptance, a sense of being at home. It's nice to hear your voice. Legs entwined, our bodies melt together.

Lights lit low. We slip through the night, gathering energy to kiss, between dips in and out of consciousness. A surge.
