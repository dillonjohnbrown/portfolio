Somewhere in the creation of this mockup I realized I borrowed a lot from the exemplary NetNewsWire. So I forked their repo on GitHub and hope to use it to build my dream macOS podcast app. Someday...

Mobile Audio Apps

There are more music apps than ever. However, few of them are made for people like me. Instead of renting music from Spotify, I buy my own copy from sites like Bandcamp. Additionally, I have other kinds of audio files, like audiobooks, stand-up comedy albums, and epic longform podcasts no longer in a feed.

I want to make a suite of apps laser-focused on one medium each. I envision these audio apps sharing a codebase for much of their functionality and similar interfaces, each tailored to their content.

Pocket Casts
Mine

This is a leading podcast app on a 4-inch iPhone screen.

My design introduces a finger-friendly waveform scrubber. The scrubber can be moved from any point across the width of the phone.

This is how Pocket Casts presents a podcast I added.

The file name is used instead of its metadata, and "Custom Episode" is displayed instead of useful information.

A talented artist put a lot of detail in the artwork, and with my design you can see it.

Swipe to the left to see show notes. An additional swipe does not move to the queue, as the icon placement would suggest.

On this screen you can not control playback, but you are informed of the full episode length — in minutes.

From the main player screen, swipe up or tap the bottom buttons for additional functionality. The scrubber and playback buttons stay on screen.

Swipe horizontally to move between the queue and settings tabs.

Music

I download music from all over the web, like Bandcamp and artist websites. One album costs as much as a month of Spotify, so why do I pay more for less?

I like the freedom to choose my music player, change file metadata, or even play a song backwards. I love knowing the artists who shape my worldview get paid more for their work.

Tap on the artwork to play or pause. Swipe left or right to change tracks.

Your queue, settings, equalizer, and lyrics are one tap away.

No algorithms, no social — just your music.

Stand-Up Comedy

I haven't found any apps made just for comedy albums. Sure, you could toss your MP3s in your music app, but that's not ideal. Comedy bits could come up when shuffling your music, and you are forced to use an interface made for music.

These apps are so dead simple they barely need mockups. Most of my mockups look the same as apps made 10 years ago. Good design is timeless afterall.

Cookbook App

I lose my place in cookbook apps and websites all the time. My idea adds colors, columns, and cleverness. Just look at those useful features — two columns so you are less likely to lose your place, color-coded ingredients, and in-line timers.
