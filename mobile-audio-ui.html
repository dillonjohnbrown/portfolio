There are more music apps than ever. However, few of them are made for people like me. Instead of renting music from Spotify, I buy my own copy from sites like Bandcamp. Additionally, I have other kinds of audio files, like audiobooks, stand-up comedy albums, and longform podcasts no longer in a feed.

I want to make a suite of apps laser-focused on one medium each. These apps will share a codebase and similar interfaces, each fine-tuned to their content.

My design introduces a large, finger-friendly scrubber. Grab the waveform from any location and slide horizontally to change your place in a song.

A large touch target makes for easy one-handed use with any grip.

Apple's Human Interface Guidelines suggest a minimum size of 44pt for interactive elements. Most popular apps don't even come close.

Are the media controls not big enough for your liking? My interface has an option to replace them.

Tap on the artwork to play/pause your music. Swipe left and right to change tracks.

The shape around the bottom buttons is a clever detail to subtly hint at something more.

The scrubber (and buttons, if you like) stay on screen after scrolling. This allows you to control playback and read lyrics at the same time.

Gestures pack in more functionality without sacrificing screen space. However, they can be hard to find. In my design, gestures are an additional way to use the interface.
